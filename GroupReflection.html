Group Reflection

Nick Parker

I believe the group performed admirably. Everyone pulled their weight, and the quality of work was better than expected. It was impressive how receptive to feedback everyone was. As we got more comfortable with each other over the weeks, I felt it was clear the dynamic was working well. It was this aspect that was most surprising.

However, there were times when communication became a problem. There were instances where some members disappeared briefly, which was concerning given a history of dropouts from our original team. In this regard it is an area for improvement, but one that is being addressed through open and honest dialogue. I felt that each of us were understanding of the circumstances and annoyances, and I believe this dialogue worked well in managing potential conflict.

If there was only one thing learnt from group work, it was how to break a complex project into clearly defined steps or sections. We may not have intentionally set out in this manner, but we certainly arrived at that point. Towards the end we kept saying to each other "after section x which I'm doing, all we have left is section y from you, and section z from him! Killing it!"

Overall, I feel we did "kill it", and I can't wait to attack the next assignment with 404.

Group

Every single member of group 404 dreaded the thought of collaborative assignments. Every member had previously been involved in groups, either at work or school, where there was conflict, non-participation, a lack of direction, or all of the above. Every single member concluded however, without hesitation that 404 worked emphatically well together. Everyone was pleased with each other’s efforts. Everyone was mature enough to come to the table with any concerns, and felt comfortable enough with each other to both give and receive helpfully critical feedback on any given task. We all agree we could not have asked for a better group.

All things considered, we each agreed that communication was sometimes an issue. To begin with we had several members who just disappeared without a trace. To compound this issue further we had an interloper. A member who was not invited, and did not helpfully contribute by any means at all. Finally, we had several participating members go AWOL briefly, which was concerning to the rest of the group given these prior issues. It became apparent that these particular members were simply unwell, and the issue was resolved. We all concluded however that notification would have benefited the group, to put everyone’s mind at ease.

Not only did we learn how important communication was, we additionally learnt how important it was to stay on task and ensure that we were all committed to the deadlines proposed. Yes, because of illness this was difficult for some, but because others had complied, we were able to keep the overall assignment completely under control. Given the fact that almost all team members have full time jobs and various family commitments, there was no point at which any of us felt overwhelmed or behind and we were each extremely surprised by this.

We were also surprised by our individual personal profile pages from assignment one. Each of us did not overly hold much regard to the results of the Myers-Briggs test, and for the most part dismissed them. As assignment 2 progressed, each of us noticed similarities between profile results and our colleagues that perhaps the individual may not have seen in themselves. It was clear that those that were outlined as organised and practical, were indeed organised and practical. Those that were altruistic and sensitive, were indeed so. Finding these similarities was surprising to a group that so casually dismissed the profile results as pseudo-science.

Going forward we are excited to learn more about each other, and about the topics at hand. Each of us have skills another may not, and together we expect great things.
